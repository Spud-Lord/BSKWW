BBC BITESIZE - FINANCE SECTION
  • Profit - A Profit is where you exceed your break even point. Your money coming in is higher than your money going out.
  • Loss - A Loss is where your final balance is lower than your break even point. Your money going out is higher than your money coming in.
  • Revenue - Revenue is the money coming into the business before expenditure is added.
  • Expenditure - Expenditure is the money that goes out of a business.
  • Cash Inflow - Cash Inflow is the same as Revenue. It is the money coming into a business.
  • Cash Outflow - Cash Outflow is the same as Expenditure. It is the money going out of the business.
  • Cashflow - Cashflow is the movement of money in and out of the business.
  • Closing Balance - Closing Balance is the money at the end of the month after Expenditure is added to Revenue.
  • Opening Balance - Opening Balance is the money at the start of the month. This will be the same as the previous months Closing Balance.
  • Net Profit - Net Profit is money before Expenditure is added.
  • Gross Profit - Gross Profit is money after Expenditure is added.
  • Sales - Sales is the money recieved from sales of the business' product or service.
  • Fixed Costs - Fixed Costs are costs that don't change depending on the sale of the business' product or service.
  • Variable Costs - Variable Costs are Costs that are affected by the sale/production of the business' product or service.
  • Break-Even Point - The Break-Even Point is where a business doesn't make a profit or a loss.
  • Margin of Safety - The Margin of Safety is where the business is making a profit but close to the Break-Even Point.
